Tech is inherently cold. It needs to be molded by a human-centered designer. This is a human-first perspective to design.

Tech is predominantly male. A woman's perspective is routinely sidelined to the detriment of us all. Tech needs to be molded by women. This is my women-first perspective to practicing UX Design and UX Research in the tech industry.

I believe the software and environments we design for people should be intuitive, usable, and delightful. I have worked at fast-paced startups, and I have consulted with Fortune 50 companies, MAANG companies, and publicly traded tech corporations.

My perspective is built for UX professionals who want to deeply mature their craft and practice while navigating a male-dominated environment.

In tech, men control the pace of work. The woman-first lens flips this paradigm completely. It requires changing the default behavior mechanics of how you show up in digital spaces daily.

Be Woman First To:
  • Enter the meeting room
  • Turn on your camera
  • Respond in lines of communication
  • Push the execution update
  • Summarize the alignment
Be Woman Last To:
  • Organize secondary administrative tasks
  • Remind others of basic responsibilities
  • Clean up administrative messes

This means forgoing default subordinate roles in favor of taking absolute control of the pace of work.
